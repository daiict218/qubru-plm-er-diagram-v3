✦ What changed from v2 → v3
v3 is derived from v2 and corrected against the ACORD framework and real operational scenarios. Same review lens — entities as nodes, every relationship carries a key. Net +2 entities (42 → 44) and a deep attribute pass (~120 → ~580+ attributes).
✓ Party / Role separation (ACORD-aligned)
  • Identity lives once in Party; roles are contextual per domain.
  • Removed Principal (→ a PartyRelationship) and Driver (→ ClaimInvolvement + Signal) — rigid roles that were really identities.
✓ Account as the commercial-relationship anchor
  • New Account ties a customer to the carrier (placed through a broker) — the spine of account-lifecycle reconstruction.
✓ Actors include AI agents
  • Actor is human or AI agent; every machine-produced fact is traceable to who produced it (ACORD 2023 "virtual parties").
✓ Combined ratio is computable
  • New CommissionSchedule supplies the expense side — without it, combined ratio (the commercial argument of PLM) can't be computed.
✓ LOB-agnostic claim parties
  • New ClaimInvolvement records who was involved in a claim (driver, injured third party, witness) as a contextual involvement, not a subtype.
✓ Bitemporal product rules
  • ProductVersion carries effective_from / effective_to — "what were the rules when we bound this policy?" is answerable.
Design decisions locked in v3
  • Party-Role separation; Account as relationship anchor; ProductVersion as bitemporal foundation.
  • Signals carry a controlled vocabulary + concern flags; Patterns & Recommendations carry disposition for the feedback loop.
  • Provenance is first-class from day one — every AI-produced fact is auditable.
